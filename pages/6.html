What is envy?

Envy is an emotion or a feeling of unhappiness or covetousness which occurs when a person wants something another person has.

For example, if your friend has a sportscar and you want to have it, this means that you’re envious of your friend.

It is a complex experience, envy can consist of many situations: feelings of inferiority, hate towards the envied person, and guilt.

You generally don’t envy those people who experience success in those work that is not similar to yours.

If you’re a doctor, and your mechanical engineer friend gets promoted, you’ll never feel envious but pleased about him.

His field and career selection are completely different from yours; you’re seeking different paths.

In the dutch, according to the possibility envy has two types; benign envy and malicious envy.

According to research, malicious envy is bad emotion that affects the envious person to want to bring down their better off even at their own cost.

Benign envy is also a negative emotion like malicious, in the sense, it also feels bad.

Research about benign envy shows that it can provide motivation, good thoughts about another person, and appreciation.

If this envy is dealt with correctly can positively affect a person’s life inspiring them to be successful person in the future.

What is jealousy?

Jealousy is a complex emotion that contains feelings that begin from suspicion to violence to terror to embarrassment.

It affects people of all ages who are associated with being overprotective of another person, such as a mate or friend.

Most people face jealousy from day to day, but too much jealousy can interfere with life. People in this situation think about speaking with a therapist.

Jealousy in people can be seen in many ways but too much jealousy causes harm to others. Hints that you’re experiencing jealousy include:

Rage towards a person, trouble in feeling happy for a companion if he gets what you wanted, in siblings when one is receiving more love and attention than the others.

There are many forms of jealousy, it can come in many situations. Jealousy in some situations includes:

Rational jealousy falls in having pure, honest doubt, specifically when you love someone and are worried about losing them.

Platonic jealousy starts from doubt about losing a partner in a relationship. A wife is angry at his husband that he is flirting with another woman.

Difference between envy and jealousy

1.     Envy is a wish to possess what others have, jealousy is doubt of losing what you possess.

2.     In literature, jealousy is always a noun, but envy can be a noun or a verb.

3.     Envy is mostly used in the desire for a thing, while jealousy can be used in desire for both person or thing.

4.     Jealousy is specifically used in the condition of romantic relationships, whereas envy or envious is not.

5.     In Christianity, jealousy is treated as fair emotion. But envy is considered one of the deadly sins.
