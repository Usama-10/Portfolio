IS IT DENGUE?

�         INTRO

Dengue is a viral disease caused by the bite of an infected mosquito mainly a female mosquito. Dengue fever is common in Asia, Latin America, and Africa. But it is very rare in the United States. Nowadays it started spreading in more areas such as Europe and some parts of the United States.

The virus of dengue has grown rapidly around the globe in the past few years.

�         HOW DOES IT TRANSMIT

When a mosquito bites a human who is infected with the dengue virus, the mosquito becomes infected with the virus from which the disease is caused. When this mosquito bites other human beings they become also infected. This virus is not transmissible from person to person through direct contact. Four serotypes of the virus that causes dengue fever are DENV-1, DENV-2, DENV-3, and DENV-4.

�         SIGNS OF HAVING A DENGUE FEVER

Many people feel no sign of having been infected with dengue fever but some symptoms indicate that they might be infected with dengue fever, these symptoms last from 2-7 days after someone has been bitten by an infected mosquito.

1.       High fever

2.       Pain behind the eyes, pain in joints muscles, or bones.

3.       Vomiting

4.       Headache

5.       Mild bleeding from the nose

Usually, most people recover within a week. For those who cannot overcome dengue fever, the symptoms become worse and life-threatening. This stage is called severe dengue, dengue hemorrhagic fever, and dengue shock syndrome. If it is not treated immediately it can cause damage to blood vessels, difficulty in breathing, irritability, and restlessness

�         HOW TO TREAT DENGUE FEVER

In normal cases, doctors suggest patients avoid dehydration, take lots of rest, and take acetaminophen and paracetamol to relieve fever and pain. Patients should not take ibuprofen and aspirin as a painkiller, this results in more bleeding. In case of severe dengue, immediately seek medical attention.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

�         PREVENTION OF DENGUE VIRUS

There are many ways in which we can reduce the risks to have dengue fever.

1.       By vaccination

2.       Avoiding mosquito bites

3.       Wearing full sleeves shirt, and long pants if sleeping outside

4.       By using screens on windows, keeping the windows shut especially at the time of dawn and dusk when the dengue mosquitoes are most active.

Vaccination is good for those areas where dengue fever is most common. although, the WHO does not suggests everyone be vaccinated when infected by the dengue mosquito. although, the best cure is to prevent mosquito bites as much as possible. If you are going to travel to that place where dengue is common you must have to take precautions such as using mosquito repellant, use of mosquito nets if sleeping outdoors, avoid to go outside in the morning and evening, clearing the standing water around the home, and avoiding camping near the water.

�         GLOBAL AFFECTION BY DENGUE VIRUS:

Before 1970, only a few countries experienced the dengue virus. but nowadays this disease is becoming more common in more than 100 countries in the world. Many outbreaks occurred around the world, and Europe is one of them. The largest number of dengue cases is recorded in 2019 in which almost all the regions were affected and in Afghanistan, it is the first time that dengue transmission is recorded.

In Latin America, brazil was the first country alone that reached nearly 1.5 million infections of dengue in 2020
